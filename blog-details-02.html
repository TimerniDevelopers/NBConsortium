The Neurodivergent Business Consortium (NBC) presents: Penny Mordaunt MP

I was a very odd child to many people in my childhood and always wondered what I did wrong. My mother was the only supporter and understood a little before puberty. She used to say to concentrate on what you can do good and you will find your way forward very easily.

I found my strength to perform well in education, and my results were satisfactory before secondary education and attempted three times to pass the exam. I did not know what was wrong with me and was in jeopardy with my family and surroundings.

Then I came into higher education in the Uk and sometimes later discovered that the Brain tumour was the reason for not remembering things and other complications.

Neurodiversity is not a disability for many of us; it is a way to think and work differently with extreme success.

– Dr Shah Siddiqui

I took a long break and started my education again as I was unsatisfied with my situation. Although that was laughter for many around me, I did not listen to them. Completed my BSc in engineering and Master of research with first class. Later found my saviour Prof. Adrian Hopgood to finish my PhD in AI.

I have always struggled throughout my life being an introvert, and due to my health, I suffer from many other disabilities. Did not get a job with an interview. Employers intend to misjudge me for my neurodiversity and other disabilities; even holding a Doctorate, my success in securing a job is limited. The interview process and the experience are not suitable for me, and it is very stressful.

Therefore, I have come up with the idea to form the Neurodivergent Business Consortium (NBC) and the first person I named Cora Burke, to join this initiative. Together we are developing initiatives (brainchild) to help people with neurodiversity.

Today we had our first event and have many well-wishers to support this initiative. Our team is growing we have Penny Plimmer, Rebecca Leppard, Jack Smith, Dr Beatriz Lopez, Dr Steven Kapp, Dr veronica Sprott, and many others to raise our voices toward neurodiversity.

We had Penny Mordaunt MP (Lord President of the Council and Leader of the House of Commons) as our chief guest, Lord Mayor of the Portsmouth City Council Cllr. Hugh Mason as Honorable Guest, Jon Adams FRSA as a panel member.

Thank you to all the guests who have made this event successful, including Shipa Ahmed Khan, Raja Ali, Rowshonara Reza, Dr Madalina-Daniela Mihailescu, Becky Warren, Amy Butler, Clair Martin, Jon Scowen, Tom Swaffer, Mark Rands, Helen Stratton MBA FCIM, Habibur Rahman, Jane Lamer, Joseph Gamlin, Steve Baker, and Thomas Hutchinson.
